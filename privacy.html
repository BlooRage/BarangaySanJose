Privacy Policy

1. Commitment to Data Privacy

Barangay San Jose is committed to protecting the privacy and personal information of all users of the Web-Based Barangay System. This Privacy Policy explains how personal data is collected, used, stored, and protected in compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173).

2. Information Collected

The system collects only information necessary for legitimate barangay operations, including:

  • Personal Information: Full name, residential address, contact number, email address, birthdate, and other relevant resident details.
  • Identification and Supporting Documents: Barangay ID information and other documents required for verification and service processing.
  • System and Transaction Records: Account registration details, login history, document requests, complaints, blotter records, appointment schedules, payment submissions, notifications, and system-generated action logs.

3. Purpose of Data Collection

Personal data collected through the system is used exclusively to:

  • Process barangay document requests, permits, and clearances.
  • Verify resident identity and eligibility for services.
  • Manage complaints, blotter reports, appointments, and transactions.
  • Send SMS or email notifications related to system activities and announcements.
  • Generate administrative reports for monitoring, auditing, and decision-making.

Data is not used for purposes outside barangay operations.

4. Data Security Measures

To protect personal information, the system implements the following security measures:

  • Encryption of sensitive data.
  • Secure authentication mechanisms and role-based access control.
  • Restricted access to personal data limited to authorized barangay officials and employees.
  • System logging and regular backups to maintain data integrity and accountability.

These measures are designed to reduce risks of unauthorized access, loss, or misuse of personal data.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Barangay San Jose does not sell or misuse personal information. Data may only be disclosed when necessary and lawful, including to:

  • Authorized barangay officials and employees for service processing.
  • Relevant local government units for official transactions.
  • Law enforcement or government authorities when required by law.

All disclosures comply with applicable data privacy laws and regulations.

6. Data Subject Rights

In accordance with RA 10173, users have the right to:

  • Access their personal data held by the system.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or outdated information.
  • Request deletion or restriction of data, subject to legal and operational requirements.
  • File a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) in case of data privacy concerns.

7. Consent

By registering and using the Web-Based Barangay System, users voluntarily provide consent to the collection, processing, and storage of their personal data as described in this Privacy Policy.

8. Updates to This Policy

Barangay San Jose reserves the right to update this Privacy Policy when necessary. Continued use of the system after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact Us

For questions or concerns regarding data privacy and protection, users may coordinate directly with Barangay San Jose through official barangay channels.
